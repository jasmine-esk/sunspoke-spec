## Context — What Sunspoke Is

Sunspoke is a voice-coaching product that listens only to the wearer. The first SKU is the **Object** — a small sculptural desk piece that captures the user's voice throughout the day and returns reflections via Slack (daily recap, weekly Read, monthly retrospective).

The software was designed by Jasmine + Michael. Your job is to make it feel like a **million-dollar object** — the kind of thing someone would buy for the beauty of it, whether or not it had software inside.

For full context read `sunspoke.md` (the product spec) and `sun-os.md` (the venture lab it sits inside).

## The Arc — Three Phases in 14 Weeks

### Phase 1 — Weeks 1-4 · Jasmine's Prototype Month

**Goal:** get ONE working sculptural Object into Jasmine's hands so she can live with it for a month and validate that the coaching is actually good.

Your tasks:

**1. Source a jeweler/fabricator for the initial rework (Week 1-2)**

This is a simple rework, not the final designer piece. Jasmine needs something good enough to test the experience — the real sculptural form comes in Phase 2.

Requirements:

- Can create a simple sculptural housing — think "beautiful small object that sits on a desk"
- Can plate, patinate, or otherwise finish the exterior in gold, silver, or bronze tone
- Can laser-engrave a subtle Sunspoke monogram
- Signs an NDA prohibiting mention of Plaud or the base hardware

Where to look:
- **Etsy** — search *"custom jewelry fabrication small batch"*, *"brass sculpture custom"*, *"industrial hardware rework"*
- **Instagram** — #customjewelrymaking, #brasswork, #smallbatchjewelry
- **Local craftspeople in NYC / LA / SF** — ask in design communities (Are.na, Dribbble, Aither's network)
- **Specific shops worth reaching:**
  - Made Solid (NYC)
  - Craighill (Brooklyn)
  - A.M. Studios (LA)
  - Bunkhouse Studio (LA)

Budget for this one prototype: **$200-500** including materials + labor

Timeline: 2 weeks from first contact to Jasmine's hands

**2. Collaborate with Michael on the enclosure ↔ electronics interface**

- Confirm the Plaud PCB and battery dimensions
- Share dimensions with the jeweler so the housing fits correctly
- QC the finished unit with Michael before handing to Jasmine (audio works, Bluetooth pairs, charging works)

**3. Start scoping packaging (in parallel, but low urgency this phase)**

- Begin researching wooden box makers, linen tray fabricators
- Build a small shortlist of packaging vendors for Phase 2 commissioning
- No commitments yet — just research

**Deliverable at end of Phase 1:**
One working prototype Object in Jasmine's hands by Week 3-4. Jasmine uses it for ~4 weeks before greenlighting Phase 2.

### Phase 2 — Weeks 5-10 · Final Object Design + Packaging + Flows

**Goal:** design the FINAL Founder's Edition Object that goes to 20 founders. This is the version that has to feel like a million-dollar product.

Your tasks:

**1. Source THE industrial designer (Weeks 5-6)**

This is not the Phase 1 jeweler. This is a proper industrial designer who can create a real sculptural object — the kind you'd see on a gallery desk or a CEO's shelf.

Requirements for the designer:
- Proven portfolio in small consumer objects, jewelry-adjacent work, or premium product design
- Can deliver from concept to manufactured prototype
- Understands the "object, not gadget" brief
- Comfortable working within Plaud NotePin internals as a technical constraint
- Signs an NDA

Where to look:
- **Designer directories:** Are.na, Designboom, Core77, It's Nice That
- **Specific designers/studios to email:**
  - Layer Design (Benjamin Hubert) — has a jewelry line
  - Map Project Office (UK) — premium consumer hardware
  - Native Union (warm premium tech accessories)
  - Bould Design (SF) — consumer electronics
  - Teenage Engineering (if budget allows and they'll do contract work)
  - Smaller independents on Are.na — search "product designer consumer objects"
- **Aither's network** — ask Jasmine for introductions from Aither's client roster

Budget for the designer: **$20-40K** (concept → CAD → prototype → final production-ready files)

Timeline: 4-6 weeks from engagement to final production-ready files

**2. Lead the sculptural form direction (Weeks 5-10)**

Work with the designer on:
- Form: sculpture, not gadget. Needs to feel like an object you'd own even if it didn't work.
- Size: ~60-80mm tall (fits the Plaud internals, feels substantial on a desk)
- Material direction: brass, sterling silver, oxidized bronze. Optional ceramic or stone inlay.
- Finish options (founders will choose one at checkout): oxidized bronze, sterling silver plate, 18k gold plate, potentially a fourth "stone inlay" premium tier
- Weight: needs to feel *heavy* — lightness reads as cheap
- Interior access: designer needs to figure out how electronics are inserted (screwed bottom plate? magnetic seam? hidden compartment?)
- Mic placement: top of the object for best audio capture
- Charging: induction pad in the base, or hidden magnetic contact — no visible USB port

**Reference objects to inspire (show these to the designer):**
- Arne Jacobsen desk clocks
- Teenage Engineering OP-1 (playful premium)
- Muji aroma diffuser (simple, self-contained, beautiful)
- Japanese netsuke (tactile small objects)
- Le Gramme numbered bracelets (weight-as-identity)
- Worry stones in precious materials
- Nomos wall clocks (minimal craft)

**3. Production of 20 final Objects (Weeks 9-10)**

Once the final design is locked:
- Commission 20 units (plus 3-5 spares for replacements/failures)
- Each unit hand-finished, engraved with serial number (01-20)
- Each founder picks one of the finish options during outreach

Production budget: **$300-600 per unit × 20 = $6,000-12,000**

**4. Design and source the packaging (Weeks 6-10)**

The unboxing is part of the product. Budget $100-200/unit on presentation.

Spec:
- **Outer box** — heavy paperboard or wooden clamshell. Debossed or laser-engraved Sunspoke monogram. No other text.
- **Inner tray** — linen or felt-lined, recessed cavity holding the Object
- **Hand-signed letter** — from Jasmine, in a matched envelope, wax seal or embossed closure
- **Cord/chain options** — in a secondary compartment (even though v0 is the Object, not the Wear, including cord + chain options signals the broader product family)
- **Single instruction card** — *"Wear it. We're listening."* + Slack invite link
- **Hidden drawer beneath the tray** — magnetic charging puck (unbranded; if it's Plaud's original, swap for a generic $8 USB-C magnetic cable)

Sourcing path — Etsy woodworker + bespoke print shop:
- **Wooden box:** Etsy or local woodworker, ~6-8 weeks, $80-150/box
- **Letter stationery + instruction card:** Jukebox Print, Moo Luxe, or a local letterpress — ~$15-20/set
- **Linen pouches for cords:** Etsy jewelry supplier, ~$3-5 each

Packaging total: **$100-200/unit × 20 = $2,000-4,000**

**5. Design the UX flows (parallel throughout Phase 2)**

While hardware is being designed, you design the Slack-based user experience. Michael implements what you design.

Design deliverables:
- **Onboarding conversation flow** — what the Sunspoke bot says in Day 1 setup. Warm, literary, not robotic. 5 minutes max to complete.
- **Daily evening recap layout** — what the Slack message looks like. Text + embedded audio. Tone of voice for the written summary.
- **Weekly Read format** — the longer Sunday message. Structure, headers, how the specificity cards look.
- **Archetype card visual** — the image attached to the Weekly Read when the user's archetype is revealed. This is a screenshot-worthy artifact. Think tarot card meets Japanese wood print meets Stripe dashboard. 10-12 archetypes need cards designed.
- **Slash commands UX** — `/sunspoke pause`, `/sunspoke goal`, `/sunspoke note` — how these feel, what confirmation looks like.
- **Error and edge case copy** — low wear day, missed charge, "this doesn't feel right" flagging affordance.

Format for deliverables: Figma + copy doc. Michael translates to code.

Iteration rhythm:
- Week 5-6: first drafts of all flows
- Week 7-8: Michael implements, Jasmine reviews against her month of usage
- Week 9-10: refine based on real user reactions

## Non-Negotiables

These are design principles to hold the line on:

1. **Object over gadget.** If it feels like tech, redesign. If it feels like jewelry or an art object, you're close.
2. **Weight matters.** Lightness reads as cheap. The Object should feel *substantial* in the hand.
3. **No plastic.** No plastic-on-plastic clicks, no plastic finishes, no plastic anything anywhere in the user-facing product.
4. **Silence.** No chimes, no beeps, no audible notifications from the hardware. Haptic only, if anything.
5. **Specificity in flow copy.** Never "You said a lot of filler words today." Always "You said 'just' 47 times today, mostly when making requests."
6. **Pull over push in flows.** Users pull insight when ready. Push is reserved for the scheduled daily/weekly artifact.
7. **Hide Plaud.** No Plaud branding anywhere on hardware, in flows, in setup, in documentation. See §12 of `sunspoke.md` for the ready answer if anyone asks.
