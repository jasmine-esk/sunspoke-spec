## Context — What Sunspoke Is

Sunspoke is a voice-coaching product that listens only to the wearer. The first SKU is the **Object** — a small sculptural desk piece that captures the user's voice throughout the day and returns reflections via Slack (daily recap, weekly Read, monthly retrospective).

The software layer is Michael. The creative direction is Jasmine + Aither. The execution — the dozens of small daily tasks that keep the project moving — is you.

For full context read `sunspoke.md` (the product spec) and `sun-os.md` (the venture lab it sits inside).

---

## Your Role — Design Intern, Research & Execution

You are the **connective tissue** between Jasmine, Aither, and Michael. You make the trains run. You do the research that unblocks senior decisions. You draft and iterate on flows. You chase vendors. You track what's shipping and what's stuck.

You are not the senior creative lead on Sunspoke — that's Aither, with Jasmine as product owner. But you'll touch every part of the product, and the quality of your research and coordination directly determines how fast this thing ships.

### What you own end-to-end
- **Vendor research and outreach:** jewelers, industrial designers, packaging vendors, fabrication shops
- **Moodboards and visual references:** for every vendor brief, every flow design, every packaging concept
- **Draft flow mockups in Figma:** first passes on Slack conversations, Weekly Read layouts, archetype cards
- **Vendor management:** NDAs, quotes, schedules, QC tracking, back-and-forth
- **Project coordination:** weekly status updates, meeting notes, handoffs between Jasmine / Aither / Michael
- **Logistics:** shipping, receiving, inventory tracking

### What you support (but don't own)
- **Final creative decisions:** those belong to Aither (design quality) and Jasmine (product direction)
- **Which industrial designer we hire:** you shortlist, Jasmine + Aither choose
- **Final packaging aesthetic:** you source candidates, Aither art-directs
- **Flow copy and final layouts:** Aither reviews and refines your drafts before handoff to Michael
- **Engineering decisions:** fully Michael's call

### Who you work with
- **Jasmine** — product owner, final decision-maker. Weekly check-in.
- **Aither** — brand + design studio, senior creative on everything. Expect to be reviewed by them on mockups.
- **Michael** — engineering lead, builds everything you design (after Aither's review). Mid-week handoff cadence.
- **External vendors** — you run point on day-to-day.

## The Arc — Three Phases in 14 Weeks

### Phase 1 — Weeks 1-4 · Jasmine's Prototype Month

**Goal:** get one working sculptural Object into Jasmine's hands so she can live with it for a month and validate that the coaching software is good.

**Status update (important):** Jasmine has already ordered a Plaud NotePin **plus several Chinese voice-recording knockoffs**. Michael will evaluate all of them in Week 1 and determine which base hardware is best for the final rework. Your sourcing work in Phase 1 is about finding the right **fabricator**, not the right base device.

Your tasks:

**1. Research and shortlist fabricators for the Phase 1 prototype (Week 1)**

Once Michael picks the base hardware, you need to find someone who can:
- Safely disassemble the device (battery handling matters)
- Create a simple sculptural housing — "small beautiful object that sits on a desk"
- Plate, patinate, or otherwise finish the exterior in gold, silver, or bronze
- Laser-engrave a subtle Sunspoke monogram
- Sign an NDA prohibiting mention of the base hardware source

**Where to look:**
- **Etsy** — *"custom brass fabrication small batch,"* *"jewelry fabricator custom order,"* *"sculpture custom metalwork"*
- **Instagram** — #customjewelrymaking, #brasswork, #smallbatchjewelry, #custommetalwork
- **Reddit:** r/metalworking, r/jewelrymaking
- **NYC / LA / SF craftspeople** — Made Solid, Craighill, A.M. Studios, Bunkhouse Studio

**Your deliverable:**
A shortlist of 3-5 fabricators with quotes, samples of their past work, and availability. Jasmine picks one.

Budget for Phase 1 prototype: **$200-500** including materials + labor.

**2. Coordinate the fabricator with Michael (Weeks 2-3)**

- Confirm the hardware dimensions (Plaud or whichever device Michael selects)
- Share dimensions with the fabricator so the housing fits
- Track the build, flag delays early
- Coordinate QC with Michael before Jasmine receives the prototype

**3. Begin vendor research for Phase 2 (ongoing)**

While the prototype is being made, start researching:
- Industrial designers for the final Object form
- Packaging vendors (wooden box makers, letterpress shops)
- Chain/cord suppliers for the presentation box

No decisions yet — just build the shortlists.

**Deliverable at end of Phase 1:**
One working prototype Object in Jasmine's hands by Week 3-4. Plus Phase 2 vendor shortlists ready for Jasmine's review.

**4. Draft the UX flows in Figma (Weeks 5-10)**

While hardware and packaging are in production, you draft the Slack-based flow experiences. **These are first drafts — Aither reviews and refines before Michael implements.**

**What to draft:**
- **Onboarding conversation** — what the Sunspoke bot says in Day 1 setup. 4 questions + voice enrollment + setup of delivery time. Tone: warm, literary, confident, never robotic.
- **Daily evening recap layout** — how the Slack message looks. Text structure + embedded audio. 8pm default delivery.
- **Weekly Read format** — Sunday message. Structure, headers, specificity cards (screenshottable moments), archetype card integration.
- **Archetype card visuals** — the image attached to the Weekly Read when the user's archetype is revealed. Tarot card × Stripe dashboard × Japanese wood print energy. 10-12 archetypes need cards. Draft 3-5 for Aither's first review.
- **Slash command UX** — `/sunspoke pause`, `/sunspoke goal`, `/sunspoke note` — how these feel, what confirmation looks like.
- **Error and edge state copy** — low wear day, missed charge, "this doesn't feel right" flagging.

**Your process:**
1. First drafts in Figma by Week 7
2. Aither review + refinement by Week 8
3. Jasmine review against her real month of usage by Week 9
4. Final versions handed to Michael by Week 10

## Non-Negotiables

Design principles to hold the line on, even in early drafts:

1. **Object over gadget.** If it feels like tech, go back and redesign. Feels like jewelry or an art object = you're close.
2. **Weight matters.** Lightness reads as cheap. The Object must feel *substantial* in hand.
3. **No plastic.** Not anywhere the user can see or touch.
4. **Silence.** No chimes, no beeps, no audible notifications from hardware. Haptic only, if anything.
5. **Specificity in flow copy.** Never *"You said a lot of filler words today."* Always *"You said 'just' 47 times today, mostly when making requests."*
6. **Pull over push.** Users pull insight when ready. Push is reserved for the scheduled daily/weekly artifact.
7. **Hide Plaud (and any other off-the-shelf hardware).** No base-hardware branding anywhere on the finished product, in flows, or in documentation. See §12 of `sunspoke.md` for the ready answer if anyone asks.
