## 0. Executive Summary

**Sunspoke** is a voice-coaching product sold as a worn object. It captures the wearer's speech throughout their day, then returns a reflection — not in real time, but in rhythms (daily, weekly, monthly). The thesis: *it only coaches you on what you said.*

The product ships in two phases:

- **v0 — Founder's Edition (Q3 2026):** A Plaud NotePin, cosmetically reworked (plated, engraved, rebranded), paired with custom coaching software delivered via Slack. 20 hand-numbered units shipped to a pre-committed cohort of successful founders. $999/unit with lifetime subscription included. Total v0 budget: ~$95-150K.

- **v1 — Public launch (2027):** Custom-built rod pendant or alternative form factor with on-device voice-ID gating. Real hardware IP, scalable manufacturing, $599-799 retail. Funded by v0 traction + friends-and-family round.

Sunspoke exists inside **Sunspell**, the venture lab spun out of Aither (design studio).

## 1. Product Positioning

### Core thesis
The wearable AI market is crowded with assistants — devices that record everyone, answer questions, interrupt conversations, surveil the room. Sunspoke is the opposite: a device that only processes the wearer's own speech, never interrupts, never surveils others, and delivers reflection in literary rhythms instead of real-time alerts.

Not an assistant. **A mirror.**

### Three competitive differentiators
1. **Only coaches you on what you said** (voice-ID coaching, even though capture happens broadly in v0)
2. **Pull over push** — no real-time nagging; four tempos (daily, weekly, monthly, yearly)
3. **Object over gadget** — worn as jewelry, presented as an heirloom, not marketed as tech

### Target users (v0)
Two personas, one product:
- **Therapists and coaches** sharpening their own clinical/communication practice
- **Founders, execs, senior leaders** whose careers compound on how they show up

### Category frame
Position near Oura, Whoop, Aesop, Le Gramme. Not near Humane, Friend, Rabbit, Limitless.

## 2. The 20 Founder Cohort Strategy

### Current state
~20 successful founders have expressed interest in Sunspoke through conversation. These are pre-market champions — some are household names in tech, some are quieter but well-networked. They are the acquisition wedge for the entire company.

### Realistic conversion expectations
Of the 20 expressed-interest founders, assume:
- **6-10 will convert to paying Founder's Edition deposits** ($100 deposit → $999 final)
- **3-5 will ghost-read and not respond** (normal)
- **2-3 will reply with interest but not commit** (follow up in 2 weeks)
- **1-2 will respond with investment interest** or introductions to 5+ friends each

### The strategic playbook
1. **Weeks 1-4 — Convert:** Send personalized invites when the website and manifesto are ready. Offer $999 Founder's Edition with lifetime subscription + monthly founder's call + first-access to Sunspoke Pair and Sunspoke Keep when they ship. Hand-numbered 01-20.

2. **Weeks 5-10 — Onboard:** As deposits arrive, give each founder access to the Slack-delivered coaching pipeline immediately (using their existing device audio / AirPods if they have them — not essential to wait for hardware). This starts the relationship and data collection early.

3. **Week 10 — Ship:** Deliver physical Founder's Editions with hand-signed letter, wooden presentation box, chain/cord options.

4. **Months 3-6 — Compound:** Weekly feedback calls with the cohort. Collect testimonials. Invite 2-3 to invest in the friends-and-family round ($200-400K) if they've expressed interest. Their usage data becomes the training signal for the coaching model.

5. **Months 6-12 — Evangelize:** Founder testimonials drive public launch. Some of them will post publicly; some will introduce friends privately. Both work.

### Investment optionality
Be ready for the question *"can I invest?"* from ~3-5 of the 20. Decide in advance:
- If raising: $15-20M SAFE cap, $200-400K total round, keep it small and strategic
- If not raising: *"Not yet — but you'll be the first call when we do."*

### Ambassador tier (optional)
2-3 highest-value/highest-reach founders can be offered a free lifetime Founder's Edition in exchange for:
- 1 piece of public content per quarter (Twitter thread, podcast mention, blog post)
- 5+ warm introductions per year
- Participation in product testing and feedback calls

## 5. UI/UX Specification

### v0 — Slack is the interface

No iOS app for v0. All coaching lives in a Slack workspace or as Slack DMs from the Sunspoke bot. This is intentional: Slack is where your founder cohort already spends their day. Reaching them there is higher-engagement than asking them to open a new app.

### Three Slack delivery surfaces

**1. The daily evening message (~8pm user-chosen time)**
- Arrives as a DM from the Sunspoke bot
- Contains:
  - Short written summary (2-3 paragraphs, specificity over generality)
  - 2-minute audio recap as playable audio attachment
  - One line connecting today's patterns to the user's chosen goal
  - A single prompt for reflection ("Anything worth noting from today?")
- User can reply with thoughts; responses feed into the next day's context

**2. The weekly Read (Sunday evening or user-chosen day)**
- Longer DM with structured sections:
  - Archetype card (PDF attachment or rendered image)
  - 3-5 paragraphs of observed patterns with direct quotes and timestamps
  - 3 specificity cards (screenshottable moments of uncanny accuracy)
  - Proposed goal refinement for the next week
  - Audio version (3 minutes)
- This is the artifact founders will screenshot and share

**3. The monthly retrospective (30 days in)**
- Compare current archetype to onboarding baseline
- Progress narrative, not metrics
- One recommendation: evolve, swap, or commit another cycle

### Optional push notifications (opt-in only)
- Post-conversation reviews ("That was an intense moment, want to review?")
- Pre-meeting primers ("Heads up: based on past patterns, tomorrow's 2pm may be one to watch")
- Haptic on the pendant itself (if Plaud firmware allows — likely not in v0)

### Onboarding flow (delivered via Slack DM conversation)

Day 1 setup conversation, ~5 minutes total:
1. Welcome message with founder's number (*"You are Founder's Edition 07"*)
2. Four questions:
   - "What should I call you?"
   - "Your pronouns?"
   - "What do you do? (one sentence)"
   - "What's one thing about how you communicate that bugs you?"
3. Voice enrollment: *"Please record this paragraph aloud so I know your voice."* (30-second reading, used for speaker diarization baseline)
4. Explanation: *"For the next 7 days I'm just listening. No coaching. On [date] your first reading arrives. Wear Sunspoke, live your life."*
5. Set evening delivery time for daily recaps

### Day 4 gentle check-in
Optional DM: *"Quick check — anything unusual this week I should know? Sick, traveling, hard stretch, great stretch?"*

### Day 7: The First Read
Push notification: *"Your first reading is ready."*
User replies with 3 reflection questions before the Read is revealed (archetype + patterns + proposed goal).

### Week 4+: Ongoing rhythm
Daily recaps, weekly reads, monthly retrospectives. User can pause anytime via `/sunspoke pause` command.

### Future iOS app (v1 — not built yet)

When custom hardware ships, port the experience to a native iOS/Android companion app with richer visual design. For v0, Slack is sufficient and actually preferable (no app install friction, meets users where they work).

## 7. Hardware Specification

### v0 Hardware — Plaud NotePin Rework

**Base unit:** Plaud NotePin
- Dimensions: 55×21×8mm
- Weight: 16.8g
- Battery: 300 minutes continuous recording, rechargeable
- Bluetooth 5.x, syncs to phone for transcription
- MSRP: $169

**Rework process (per unit):**
1. Disassemble carefully (battery safety critical)
2. Remove existing enclosure
3. Electroplate enclosure:
   - **Finish A:** Sterling silver plate (3-5 microns for durability)
   - **Finish B:** 18k gold plate, brushed finish (3-5 microns)
   - **Finish C:** Blackened silver / gunmetal finish (PVD or oxidized silver)
4. Laser-engrave on back:
   - Sunspoke monogram (small, subtle)
   - Serial number (01-20)
   - Optional: founder's initial
5. Reassemble
6. Full QC: test audio capture, Bluetooth pairing, battery cycle, recording integrity
7. Package

**Per-unit cost:**
- Plaud NotePin: $169
- Rework labor + plating + engraving: $200-400
- Packaging and presentation: $100-200
- **Total: $469-769 per unit**

**Total hardware cost for 20 units:** ~$9,400-15,400

**Target spec:**
- Form factor: TBD — final decision between rod pendant, carry stone, or modular
- Dimensions: ~42×11×7mm (rod) or ~35×25×10mm (carry stone)

## 8. Tech Specification — Michael's Build

### v0 Stack Overview

```
[Rebranded Plaud NotePin]
    ↓ records audio (everything, unfiltered)
[Plaud's cloud]
    ↓ transcribes all speech
[Michael's Backend Service]
    ├── Pulls transcripts via Plaud API or export pipeline
    ├── Speaker diarization (Pyannote or similar) — identifies wearer
    ├── Filters to wearer-only speech segments
    ├── Pattern analysis and context accumulation
    ├── Calls Claude API with structured prompts
    ├── Generates daily recap, weekly Read, monthly retrospective
    ↓
[Slack Bot — Sunspoke]
    ├── Delivers daily messages (evening, user-chosen time)
    ├── Delivers weekly Read with archetype card
    ├── Handles conversation (onboarding, pause commands, feedback)
```

### Technology choices

- **Language / runtime:** Python (best ML ecosystem) or Node.js (faster for Slack bot)
- **LLM:** Claude API (Opus for weekly Reads, Sonnet for daily recaps to manage cost)
- **Speaker diarization:** Pyannote-audio (open source) or commercial (Pindrop, Rev)
- **Audio transcription:** Rely on Plaud's transcription for v0; evaluate self-hosted Whisper in v0.5
- **Database:** Postgres for user data, pattern history, readouts
- **Hosting:** Fly.io, Railway, or AWS Lambda — keep it cheap, not scale-optimized
- **Slack integration:** Bolt-js or Bolt-python framework
- **Secrets management:** 1Password SDK or Doppler
- **Monitoring:** Simple (Sentry + Plausible). Don't over-invest in observability at 20 users.

### Week-by-week build plan (6 weeks)

**Week 1 — Plaud investigation + foundation**
- Verify Plaud API availability: public, enterprise, or export-only
- Set up Plaud Pro/Enterprise account for access
- Document audio + transcript data flow
- Initialize project repo, secrets, deployment target
- **Deliverable:** one-pager answering "what can we pull from Plaud and how"

**Week 2 — Transcript pipeline + diarization**
- Build transcript ingestion (webhook or polling)
- Integrate speaker diarization
- Implement wearer-voice identification (based on 30-second enrollment sample)
- Filter transcript to wearer-only segments
- Store cleaned transcripts in Postgres with timestamps
- **Deliverable:** for one test user, output a clean "only-you" transcript from a day's recording

**Week 3 — Coaching pipeline**
- Prompt engineering for daily recap, weekly Read, monthly retrospective
- Pattern detection across transcripts (hedging words, interruption patterns, topic switches, silence lengths)
- Archetype classification logic (10-12 archetypes, see brand spec)
- Context accumulation per user (Claude's context window handles week-of-data)
- **Deliverable:** example weekly Read generated from one week of real transcripts

**Week 4 — Slack bot + delivery**
- Slack app setup (Sunspoke bot)
- Bot install flow for each founder's Slack workspace
- Daily DM delivery at user-chosen time
- Weekly Read delivery with archetype card attachment (generated as image via Python + PIL or using a template service)
- Commands: `/sunspoke pause`, `/sunspoke goal`, `/sunspoke note [text]`
- **Deliverable:** end-to-end working demo in Sunspell's own Slack

**Week 5 — Onboarding + voice enrollment**
- Onboarding conversation flow (4 questions + voice enrollment)
- Day 4 check-in logic
- Day 7 first-Read reveal ceremony
- Goal confirmation flow
- **Deliverable:** a new user can fully onboard themselves

**Week 6 — Alpha test + iteration**
- Alpha with 1-2 Aither managers first (internal dogfood)
- Bug fixes, prompt refinement, coaching quality iteration
- Performance + cost monitoring (API spend per user)
- Documentation for onboarding new founder users
- **Deliverable:** platform ready to onboard first 5 founder depositors

### Ongoing engineering (months 2-6)

- Prompt engineering based on founder feedback (30% of Michael's time)
- Coaching model iteration (new archetypes, pattern detection improvements)
- Platform hardening (error handling, reliability)
- v0.5 improvements: optional iOS app, post-conversation review, haptic triggers if Plaud firmware allows

### Engineering budget

- Michael contract/salary for v0 build: $15-20K/mo × 2 months = $30-40K
- Ongoing engineering (months 3-6): $15-20K/mo × 4 = $60-80K
- Cloud + API costs (Claude, Plaud subs, hosting): $500-1000/mo scaling with users
- **v0 engineering total: ~$30-45K**
- **First year ongoing: ~$100-130K**

## 10. "Hide Plaud" Strategy

### Three levels of concealment

**Level 1 — Cosmetic (easy, high impact)**
- Rework the enclosure (plating, engraving)
- Remove all Plaud-branded text, logos, and markings
- Replace with Sunspoke monogram and serial number
- Custom packaging with no Plaud references
- **Reach: 95% of users will never look closer**

**Level 2 — Functional (medium effort, important)**
- All coaching delivery via Slack (users never open Plaud's app)
- Michael's backend pulls transcripts invisibly from Plaud's cloud
- Setup instructions don't mention Plaud
- Bluetooth device name shows as "Sunspoke" if firmware allows renaming (check with Plaud docs)
- **Reach: 98% concealment**

**Level 3 — Impossible without custom hardware**
- FCC ID on the device maps to Plaud's filing (public record)
- Firmware identifiers, SoC model, internal PCB markings all identifiably Plaud
- Sophisticated founders who open the device will figure it out
- **Reach: cannot fully conceal from a determined investigator**

### Deliberate disclosure in legal copy
ToS includes one line: *"Sunspoke v0 is built on validated third-party voice capture hardware. Future generations will be built on proprietary hardware developed in-house."*

This protects you legally without advertising Plaud. If someone asks, you have a ready answer.

### The ready answer

If a founder asks directly *"is this a Plaud?"* — don't lie, don't panic:

*"We're using hardware from a validated third-party supplier for v0 while we finalize our own. Every component in the first generation is the best available today. The software, the coaching, the brand, the design — all ours. Custom hardware ships in 2027, and every Founder's Edition owner gets a free upgrade when it does."*

Founders respect resourcefulness more than they respect performative hardware ownership.

### Jeweler NDA
Include a simple NDA with the rework jeweler:
- No mention of Plaud or the base hardware type
- No public posting of work photos that identify the original
- Standard confidentiality, no assignment of IP

### What NOT to do
- Don't claim "custom hardware" in v0 marketing copy
- Don't say "built from scratch" or "our own hardware"
- Don't print Plaud's FCC ID anywhere visible on the device
- Don't ship a unit with original Plaud packaging or accessories

## 11. Partnership Strategy with the 20 Founders

We've scoped two viable paths for the 20 founders. Both are v0-specific; public launch moves to the standard pricing structure (see §11.3).

#### Path A — Pure Gift (Option 1)

The 20 founders receive Sunspoke as a **gift**. Zero dollars exchanged at any point.

**What they receive:**
- Founder's Edition hardware (numbered 01-20, hand-finished, their choice of finish)
- Lifetime subscription — no recurring fees, ever
- Founder's Circle membership — monthly founder's call, private Slack access, direct line to founder
- First-access to Sunspoke Pair and Sunspoke Keep when they ship
- Their unit is theirs forever

**What they commit to (ambassador contract):**
- One public endorsement on LinkedIn between Day 14-30 (while they still have the device — timing matters for authenticity)
- Weekly 20-minute feedback call during Month 1
- Permission to quote their feedback in future marketing
- Optional: one additional piece of public content per quarter (tweet, podcast mention, blog post)

**Framing for the 20:** *"You're not a customer. You're a co-creator. This is how we build."*

**Strengths:**
- Zero friction — nearly every founder will say yes
- Creates permanent loyalty ("she gave this to me because she believed in me")
- Numbered 01-20 carries real meaning (they own the artifact forever)

#### Path B — Convert-or-Return at Day 30 (Option 3)

The 20 founders receive Sunspoke on loan for 30 days. At Day 30, they choose to keep it or return it.

**What they receive for Month 1 (free):**
- Founder's Edition hardware (numbered 01-20, hand-finished)
- Full coaching pipeline access
- Weekly feedback calls with founder

**What they commit to (ambassador contract):**
- One public endorsement on LinkedIn in Weeks 3-4 (while still using the device)
- Weekly feedback calls during Month 1
- Day 30 decision: keep or return

**At Day 30, the founder chooses:**
- **Option A — Convert to keep:** $499, retains Founder's Edition unit (numbered 01-20), lifetime subscription, Founder's Circle membership
- **Option B — Return:** No charge. Hardware returns to the pipeline for Wave 2. Still keeps Founder's Circle status — their number 01-20 is held for them on a new unit whenever they want at the public launch, with $0 deposit required

Either choice keeps them in the Circle forever.

**Ambassador contract language:**
> *"You'll receive Sunspoke for 30 days at no cost. In return, you'll publish one public endorsement on LinkedIn or equivalent platform in Weeks 3-4, and participate in weekly feedback calls. At Day 30, you can choose to keep your Founder's Edition unit for $499 (with lifetime subscription and Founder's Circle membership included), or return it at no charge. Either choice keeps you in the Founder's Circle and reserves your numbered slot (01-20) on any future Sunspoke product."*

**Strengths:**
- Revenue upside at Day 30 (estimated 50-70% convert = $3,500-7,000)
- Recovered hardware (30-50%) cycles into Wave 2 of testers (therapists, sales leaders, etc.) on the same 20 units
- Clear, enforceable contract with defined decision point
- Preserves the numbered Founder's Edition mystique — they earn their unit by converting
- Operational ability to run multiple waves of testing on finite inventory

**Weaknesses:**
- Day 30 "keep or return" moment can feel transactional with peers
- Some will keep it out of social obligation, then disengage
- Some will return it and lose the emotional connection; conversion at public launch becomes harder
- More operational overhead (shipping labels, refurbishment, tracking)
- LinkedIn posts have narrower authenticity window (must land in Weeks 3-4)

**Revenue implication:** $3,500-7,000 in v0 revenue from conversions. Modest offset to the build cost.

## 12. Legal and IP

### Corporate structure
- **Entity:** Delaware C-Corporation (required for future fundraising)
- **Setup:** Clerky or Stripe Atlas (~$2-5K)
- **Timing:** Incorporate in Week 1 before any deposits are taken
- **Cap table:** Founder initially owns 100%; carve out 10% option pool for future hires

### Provisional patent
- **What to patent:** Voice-ID coaching software architecture (the filter-before-analysis approach, the archetype classification, the specific combination of pattern detection methods)
- **What not to patent yet:** Hardware design (will refile with v1's custom hardware)
- **Cost:** $15-25K through a firm like Wilson Sonsini, Fenwick, or Cooley
- **Timing:** File within 60 days of v0 ship to establish priority date

### Privacy architecture review
Critical decisions requiring lawyer input:
1. **Where does audio get processed and stored?** (Plaud's cloud, Michael's backend, users' devices)
2. **Data retention policy:** keep forever (user-controlled) or time-limited?
3. **Legacy contact mechanism:** who inherits data if a user dies?
4. **Two-party consent states:** how is this addressed in ToS and user onboarding?
5. **Right to export, right to delete, right to portability**

### Terms of Service (v0)
Draft should include:
- Hardware source disclosure (the "validated third-party" line)
- Audio capture and processing disclosure
- Voice-ID filtering explanation (what's captured vs. what's analyzed)
- Two-party consent warning (users must disclose to others)
- Data retention policy
- Legacy contact designation flow
- Subscription terms (lifetime for Founder's Edition)
- Warranty disclaimers (modified hardware, no Plaud warranty)

### Privacy Policy
Must cover:
- What audio we record
- Where it's stored and for how long
- Who has access
- How users can export or delete their data
- Third-party services involved (Plaud, Claude/Anthropic, Slack, Stripe)
- State-specific disclosures (CA CCPA, EU GDPR if applicable)

- Trademark filing for "Sunspoke" (~$1-2K, ~9 months to register)
